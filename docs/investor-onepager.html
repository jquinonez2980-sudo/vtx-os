AI Accounting Infrastructure · Canadian SMBs

From a shoebox of bank statements to filed books — with a human approving, not keying.

AcumenAI ingests a client's bank statements by email, extracts and categorizes every transaction, reconciles it against the bank's own balance, and posts approved entries straight into Sage 50 — with an immutable audit trail on every step.

● Live today on real client books — not a synthetic prototype
The Problem

Bookkeeping for Canadian SMBs is still manual, late, and error-prone.

Bookkeepers spend hours each month keying PDF and scanned bank statements into Sage 50 by hand — mis-signing transactions, missing cheques, and racing HST deadlines. The data is already structured; the bottleneck is extraction, categorization, and trust.

The bank's running balance is ground truth. AcumenAI uses it to mathematically verify every transaction — catching the sign-flips and dropped rows that manual entry misses.
The Product

Email a statement in. Approve the result. The books are done.

A registry-driven pipeline routes each statement to the right client by its account number — no manual sorting, and never one client's money booked into another's ledger.

Why It Defends

The moat is trust and accumulated client knowledge.

◆Immutable audit trail

Every action writes to an append-only log — never silently fails. The compliance story regulators and accountants require.

◆Balance-chain verification

Math, not heuristics. The bank's own balance proves each entry's sign and amount before anything is booked.

◆Per-client learned rulesets

Each client's categorization gets smarter over time and is portable across their banks. Switching cost compounds.

◆Multi-agent architecture

Nine specialized agents (ingest, reconcile, HST, RAG, comms…) coordinating over Google's ADK — extensible to new tax forms and exports.
